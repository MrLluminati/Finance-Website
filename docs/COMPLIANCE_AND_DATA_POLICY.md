# Compliance and Data Use Policy

## Purpose

This document defines the initial legal, ethical, and data-use boundaries for the finance platform.

The project should be built as a trust-first financial information and education platform. It should avoid regulatory, data-licensing, and intellectual-property risks during early development.

---

# 1. Core Compliance Position

The platform should initially provide:

- financial education;
- market explainers;
- company information summaries;
- calculators;
- public-source explainers;
- non-advisory financial content.

The platform should not initially provide:

- investment advice;
- stock tips;
- buy/sell/hold calls;
- target prices;
- personalized securities recommendations;
- real-time exchange data without licensing;
- scraped exchange data;
- copied third-party articles or protected databases.

---

# 2. Investment Advice and Research Risk

If the platform publishes securities recommendations, target prices, model portfolios, or research calls, it may trigger regulatory obligations.

Until proper legal and regulatory review is completed, all content should remain educational and informational.

Suggested page disclaimer:

> The information on this platform is provided for educational and informational purposes only. It is not investment advice, legal advice, tax advice, or a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any security or financial product. Users should conduct their own research and consult qualified professionals before making financial decisions.

---

# 3. Market Data Policy

## Early Stage

Use:

- manually curated company data;
- publicly available official documents;
- educational examples;
- delayed/free APIs only where terms permit;
- user-entered portfolio values;
- clearly labelled sample/demo data.

Avoid:

- scraping NSE/BSE pages;
- bypassing API limits;
- presenting unlicensed data as real-time;
- redistributing data from sources whose terms prohibit it;
- copying datasets from competitors.

## Future Stage

For real-time market data, obtain proper licensed feeds from authorized exchanges/data vendors.

---

# 4. Editorial Standards

Every article or company page should aim to include:

- source references;
- last updated date;
- clear separation between fact, interpretation, and opinion;
- no guaranteed-return language;
- no manipulative headlines;
- no fear-based or greed-based framing;
- no undisclosed paid promotion.

---

# 5. Content Copying Policy

Do not copy Moneycontrol, Screener, Economic Times, NSE, BSE, broker platforms, or other protected websites.

Allowed:

- original writing;
- short quotations where legally permitted;
- summaries of official public documents;
- linking to source material;
- independently created UI inspired by general finance-dashboard conventions.

Not allowed:

- copying articles;
- copying layouts pixel-for-pixel;
- copying proprietary data tables;
- copying logos/branding/trade dress;
- scraping and republishing protected content.

---

# 6. Advertising and Monetization Caution

Before monetization, review:

- ad policies;
- affiliate disclosure requirements;
- sponsored content disclosure;
- finance-sector advertising restrictions;
- investment-advice boundaries;
- privacy policy requirements.

All affiliate links or sponsorships should be disclosed clearly.

---

# 7. Privacy and User Data

Once user accounts are added, the platform must treat user financial data carefully.

Minimum practices:

- collect only necessary data;
- store passwords through trusted auth provider only;
- do not store sensitive credentials;
- do not ask for broker passwords;
- provide privacy policy;
- allow users to delete their account/data where possible;
- avoid selling personal financial data.

---

# 8. Trust Rules

The platform should follow these rules:

1. Cite sources wherever practical.
2. Show last updated date.
3. Do not misrepresent stale data as live data.
4. Do not make guaranteed return claims.
5. Do not publish fake urgency.
6. Do not recommend securities without compliance review.
7. Clearly label opinion, education, and data.
8. Use conservative language in financial content.
9. Build credibility through accuracy, not hype.
10. Maintain transparent disclaimers.

---

# 9. Review Before Launch

Before public launch beyond a portfolio/demo stage, review:

- data source terms;
- privacy policy;
- terms of use;
- disclaimer;
- advertising disclosures;
- SEBI-related risks;
- copyright/trademark issues;
- user-generated content risks, if any.

---

# 10. Current Project Rule

Until changed by explicit project decision:

> This project is an educational financial information platform and software-development portfolio project. It is not an investment advisory platform, trading platform, broker platform, or real-time exchange data distributor.
